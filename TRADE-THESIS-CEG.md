If FERC requires full cost socialization for grid upgrades or mandates metered interconnection for all large industrial loads, the economics of co-located nuclear-to-data center deals could erode or stall. Any pause in hyperscaler deal signings would remove the primary growth narrative driving CEG's premium valuation.

**Probability:** Medium (30% estimated likelihood of significant rule change in 12 months)
**Downside Impact:** Multiples could de-rate from 25x to 20x forward P/E → implied price $230 (-20%). The AI power story is >30% of CEG's premium valuation over sector peers.
**Mitigation:** CEG has secured behind-the-meter deals before FERC rules crystallized; existing PPAs appear grandfathered. Vistra Corp faces the same risk, limiting relative damage.

### Risk 3: Policy Risk — Nuclear Support Reversed or Delayed

Nuclear plants are capital-intensive to maintain; any unexpected outage at a major plant (e.g., Braidwood at 2,386 MW) could reduce annual EPS by $0.50–$1.50. Insider net selling of $99M in the past 12 months — while partly related to options exercises — is a yellow flag worth monitoring.

**Probability:** Low (15% for major policy reversal; 25% for single-plant outage exceeding guidance)
**Downside Impact:** Single major plant outage: -$0.50 to -$1.50 EPS → $7–$21 share price impact. IRA PTC reversal: potentially $2–$3 EPS impact → stock could fall to $210–$230.
**Mitigation:** Bipartisan support for nuclear remains strong; 23-reactor diversification limits single-plant concentration. CEG carries $3.6B cash and outage insurance.
