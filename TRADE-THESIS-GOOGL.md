At the current $20B quarterly run-rate, Cloud alone is generating an $80B annualized revenue stream and is already contributing meaningfully to consolidated operating margin.

**Evidence:** Q1 2026 Cloud revenue $20.0B (+63% YoY); backlog >$460B as of Q1 2026 close.

**Evidence:** Google Cloud Next (April 2026): 8th-gen TPU announcement, Blackstone $5B JV commitment, management guidance that TPU revenue begins recognizing in H2 2026.

## 2. Bear Case

### Risk 1: $175–185B Capex Bet on AI Infrastructure Could Impair Returns

Alphabet has committed $175–185B in capital expenditures for 2026, a figure that would make it the largest corporate capex program in history. Depreciation rose 38% in 2025 to $21.1B and will accelerate sharply. If AI workload demand plateaus, enterprises delay cloud migrations, or hyperscaler competitors oversupply the market, Alphabet could find itself with hundreds of billions in stranded assets generating returns well below cost of capital. FCF was $73.3B in FY2025 but this metric will be under severe pressure as capex scales.

**Probability:** Medium (30% estimated likelihood of meaningful impairment)
**Downside Impact:** FCF compression by 30–50% would pressure the FCF-yield valuation case; could send stock toward $300–320 (a -20% decline from current).
**Mitigation:** $460B Cloud backlog provides substantial demand visibility; $5B Blackstone JV offloads some capex risk; TPU external sales shift some infrastructure cost to customers.

---

### Risk 2: DOJ Antitrust Could Force Structural Remedies

The DOJ's search monopoly case resulted in a final judgment in December 2025 that banned exclusive search distribution contracts and required limited search data sharing. Both Google's appeal and the DOJ's cross-appeal (seeking forced divestitures including possibly Chrome) are before the D.C. Circuit as of 2026. Separately, the adtech remedies ruling (following a November 2025 trial) could force an AdX divestiture. EU antitrust matters — including data-sharing mandates and default agreement requirements — add additional jurisdictional risk. A Chrome or AdX spinoff is the worst-case structural scenario, potentially disrupting the data flywheel that underpins Search monetization.

**Probability:** Medium (25% chance of material structural remedy, 60% chance of behavioral-only outcome)
**Downside Impact:** AdX divestiture could remove an estimated $10–15B of high-margin ad revenue; Chrome divestiture could reduce Search share by 5–10 percentage points over 3–5 years. Combined worst case: stock declines to $280–300 (-26% to -30%).
**Mitigation:** U.S. regulatory environment under the current administration is less aggressive than prior DOJ leadership; behavioral remedies (data sharing, no exclusivity contracts) are already implemented and manageable; Chrome is deeply integrated with Android ecosystem making divestiture technically complex.

---

### Risk 3: Search Market Share Erosion Accelerates

Google's global search market share declined from 93% in 2023 to ~90% in 2026 — primarily due to AI-native search interfaces like ChatGPT Search and Perplexity. If this erosion accelerates to 85% by 2028 and share of global search ad spend follows, the impact on the ~$120B+ Search revenue base could be severe. The risk is not near-term (Search grew 19% in Q1 2026) but longer-term as Gen Z shifts default search behavior.

**Probability:** Low-Medium (15% chance of accelerated erosion to <85% share within 3 years; 50% chance of continued gradual erosion manageable at current growth rates)
**Downside Impact:** Each 1% of search market share loss represents approximately $1.2–1.5B of revenue risk annually at scale; a 5% share loss over 3 years = ~$6–7B revenue headwind.
**Mitigation:** Alphabet's AI Overviews, Gemini integration, and NotebookLM are actively winning back AI-native users; YouTube and Maps provide search adjacencies that ChatGPT cannot replicate; Android's 3 billion device install base makes Google the default entry point for mobile search globally.
