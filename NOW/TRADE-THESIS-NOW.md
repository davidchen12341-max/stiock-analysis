## 2. Bear Case

### Risk 1: Armis Integration Costs Compress Margins Longer Than Expected

ServiceNow closed the $7.75B Armis acquisition in April 2026, the company's largest acquisition ever. Integration of Armis (cybersecurity asset intelligence) and Veza (identity governance) into the ServiceNow platform is complex and costly. Management guided 81.5% subscription gross margin for FY2026 — below prior expectations — with integration costs explicitly cited as the driver. If the integration takes 18–24 months instead of 12 months, or if cross-sell synergies disappoint, the operating margin profile could remain compressed through FY2027, delaying the re-rating catalyst. At 24.83x forward P/E with margin headwinds, the multiple expansion thesis fails if margins stay compressed.

**Probability:** Medium (35% estimated likelihood)
**Downside Impact:** If operating margin stays at 28–29% through FY2027, street estimates get cut and the stock re-tests $81–$88 (prior 52-week low area).
**Mitigation:** Armis already had existing integrations with ServiceNow workflows pre-acquisition, reducing pure technical integration risk. The Autonomous Security & Risk platform launched at Knowledge 2026 suggests the product roadmap is tracking on schedule.

### Risk 2: Valuation Multiple Remains Compressed in Higher-for-Longer Rate Environment

NOW traded at a 5-year average forward P/E of approximately 60x before 2026; it currently trades at ~24.83x. While this represents massive compression, the higher-for-longer interest rate narrative could continue to suppress growth stock multiples, particularly if macro data shows persistent inflation in H2 2026. At 24x forward earnings, NOW is cheap relative to its own history but not necessarily cheap in an absolute sense for a company with near-term margin headwinds. A further de-rating to 20x forward P/E on FY2026 EPS of ~$4.10 would put the stock at $82, near the 52-week low.

**Probability:** Medium (30% estimated likelihood)
**Downside Impact:** Re-test of $81–$85 range (-17% to -21% from $102) in a macro risk-off environment.
**Mitigation:** CEO Bill McDermott purchased shares at $104.60 and $105.96 in February 2026, signaling insider confidence at current levels. Institutional ownership remains 91%+, suggesting professional investors view current levels as attractive.

### Risk 3: AI-Native Competition Disrupts Pricing Power and Expansion Economics

Microsoft (Copilot for ITSM), Salesforce (Agentforce), and emerging AI-native startups are all attacking the enterprise workflow automation space with AI-first products. If enterprises begin to view AI-native alternatives as functionally equivalent to ServiceNow's mature platform at lower price points, it could slow NOW's average contract value expansion and new logo growth. The 2026 software market reset partly reflects investor concern that AI productivity tools will compress seat growth and pricing for mature SaaS platforms. ServiceNow's expansion into cybersecurity (Armis) and supply chain (FedEx) suggests management is aware of this threat and is widening the moat, but execution risk is real.

**Probability:** Low-Medium (20% estimated likelihood for meaningful impact in next 12 months)
**Downside Impact:** If revenue growth decelerates to 15–17% by FY2027 vs. the current 21% trajectory, the stock could compress to $75–$85 range.
**Mitigation:** ServiceNow's 98% renewal rate, $28.2B RPO backlog, and 20+ year workflow integration depth make wholesale displacement extremely difficult. No competitor currently offers comparable enterprise workflow governance at scale.

**Bear Case Price Target:** $78 (-24% downside from $102)
**Bear Case Basis:** Applying 19x forward P/E to stressed FY2027 non-GAAP EPS of ~$4.10 assuming margin compression persists and revenue growth decelerates to 16–17%, reflecting a scenario where both Armis integration disappoints and macro headwinds intensify.
