- **Potential base formation:** The $81–$85 zone appears to be establishing as a longer-term support base. A successful retest and hold of that level would be constructive for a multi-month recovery thesis.

## THREE KEY FINDINGS

### Finding 1: Short-Term Momentum Has Genuinely Turned Bullish — But Volume Does Not Confirm
The MACD crossover (+1.77 to +2.40), RSI at 55–58 (recovered from oversold), and ADX confirming directional strength (+DI 37.24 vs. -DI 16.86) represent real momentum improvement. However, the recovery rally is executing on 25% below-average volume (~23.6M vs. 31.3M average), and on May 22, price rose while volume fell — a classic bearish divergence. This mismatch raises the probability that the rally is a low-conviction bounce rather than the start of a sustained reversal. Confirmation would require a high-volume (>40M shares) breakout above $103–$104.

### Finding 2: The 200-Day MA at $123–$142 Is an Immovable Ceiling That Defines the Long-Term Bear Trend
Regardless of short-term signals, NOW's 200-day moving average — the gold standard of trend determination — sits 23–43% above current prices. The stock has fallen so far that even reclaiming the 200-day MA would be a monumental achievement requiring months of sustained buying. Until NOW can at least reclaim the 100-day MA ($101–$104, right where price is stalling now), all short-term bullish signals must be viewed skeptically. The death cross (50-day below 200-day) further confirms the structural bear trend.

### Finding 3: Bull Flag at Critical Juncture — $103–$104 Is the Make-or-Break Level
NOW is forming a textbook bull flag pattern immediately below a cluster of resistance: the $103.07 immediate resistance level and the ~$104 100-day EMA. A convincing, high-volume close above $104 would: (a) confirm the bull flag breakout with a measured target of $118–$121, (b) clear the 100-day MA resistance, and (c) shift the near-term trend decisively bullish. Failure to break out — especially if the stock drifts back below the 8-day SMA at $97.87 — would suggest the pattern is failing, with risk of a retest of the $85–$90 support zone or even the $81.24 52-week low.
